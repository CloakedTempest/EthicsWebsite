Making Decisions With Algorithms

For Arguments

  • Using computers running algorithms can speed up processes such as, deciding whether a bank would benefit from giving a client a mortage or not. This is both convenient for the customer and the company.

  • There is no human error. The decision the algorithm makes cannot be argued against since it is entirely based upon statistics and real data.

  • They are significantly cheaper than paying humans to make the descisions.

Against Arguments

  • Algorithms can have biases if they are trained on a biased data set. sometimes it can be difficult to recognise a biased data set, especially if the programmers working on it are not diverse in their experiences.

  • Biases in the Algorithm can change its input. This can have a runnaway effect on the biases of the algorithm, making it even less acurrate.

  • Algorithms also struggle to handle situations they haven't been trained on whereas humans are good at improvising since they understand why they do their job.
